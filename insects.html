* CENTIPEDE *

  1. Legs: Many pairs of legs per segment, equipped with venomous claws.

  2. Speed: Fast-moving nocturnal predators active in diverse habitats worldwide.

  3. Appearance: Typically brownish or reddish with elongated bodies.

  4. Habitat: Found in forests, deserts, and caves, preferring moist environments.

  5. Behavior: Nocturnal hunters, hiding during the day.

* SPOTTED LANTERNFLY *

  1. Origin: Native to Asia, invasive in parts of North America, including the eastern United States.

  2. Appearance: Grayish with black spots (adults); black with white spots; about 1 inch long.

  3. Diet: Feeds on sap from grapevines, fruit trees, and other plants, impacting agriculture.

  4. Impact: Significant threat to crops, capable of spreading plant diseases.

  5. Control: Managed through monitoring, trapping, and chemical control methods.

* FLANNEL MOTH CATERPILLAR *

  1. Appearance:Covered in dense, irritant hairs for defense.

  2. Defense: Hairs deter predators; found in southern North America and Central America.

  3. Life Cycle:Feeds on plants, later pupates into moths.

  4. Habitat: Thrives in forests, gardens, and urban green spaces.

  5. Role: Provides food for predators while aiding in nutrient cycling.

* LYSTRA LANATA *

  1. Common Name:Also known as the Woolly Bear Caterpillar.

  2. Appearance: Covered in dense, fuzzy hairs varying from brown to black and orange.

  3. Life Cycle: Feeds on plants before pupating into a moth.

  4. Distribution: Plays a role in folklore where its bands are thought to predict winter severity.

  5. Symbolism: Folklore suggests bands predict winter severity.

* JUMPING SPIDER *

  1. Size: Small to medium-sized with exceptional jumping abilities.

  2. Vision: Large, forward-facing eyes for depth perception.

  3. Prey: Hunts insects and spiders by stalking and pouncing.

  4. Habitat: Found worldwide in various environments.

  5. Behavior: Agile hunters using silk for safety and retreats.

* TARANTULA *

  1. Appearance: Large, hairy spiders with robust bodies and distinctive markings.

  2. Habitat: Found in the dense rainforests of the Amazon Basin, often in burrows or tree hollows.

  3. Diet: Carnivorous, feeding on insects, small vertebrates, and occasionally other spiders.

  4. Behavior: Nocturnal and solitary, using ambush tactics to capture prey.

  5. Defense: Can flick urticating hairs from abdomen as a defense mechanism.

* CITHAERIAS *

  1. Common Name: Also known as the metalmark butterfly.

  2. Appearance: Vibrant metallic wings with intricate patterns.

  3. Habitat: Found in the diverse ecosystems of the Amazon rainforest.

  4. Life Cycle: Larvae feed on specific host plants before transforming.

  5. Role: Important for pollination and as prey in rainforest food webs.

* BUSH CRICKETS *

  1. Appearance: Green or brown with long antennae and leaf-like wings.

  2. Sound: Males chirp loudly by rubbing wings to attract females.

  3. Habitat: Found in grasslands, forests, and gardens worldwide.

  4. Diet: Herbivorous, feeding on leaves, flowers, and sometimes other insects.

  5. Life Cycle: Eggs laid in soil or plant stems; nymphs undergo incomplete metamorphosis.

* HERCULES BEETLE *

  1. Size: One of the largest beetles with impressive horned males.

  2. Strength: Capable of lifting objects many times its weight.

  3. Habitat: Found in tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

  4. Life Cycle: Larvae feed on decaying wood; adults feed on sap and fruit.

  5. Conservation: Some species threatened by habitat loss.
